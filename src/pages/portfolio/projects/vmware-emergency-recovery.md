**Client:** National Insurance Trust Fund  
**Project Duration:** 19/08/2025 – 20/08/2025

## Overview

Led a critical emergency response effort to recover enterprise infrastructure following a severe environmental hazard. A localized HVAC failure resulted in direct water leakage onto a production VMware cluster, causing sudden hardware failures. Acted immediately to assess the physical damage, safely remediate the affected hardware, and successfully restore both the physical servers and the critical customer data housed within the virtual environment, all within a 48-hour window.

## Key Features & Contributions

### 🚨 Rapid Incident Response
Executed an immediate emergency protocol to power down, isolate, and secure the water-damaged hardware, preventing further electrical shorting and permanent data loss.

### 🛠️ Hardware Remediation
Carefully dismantled, inspected, and restored the physical components of two affected servers, ensuring the hardware was safe and fully operational before attempting to power it back on.

### 🧊 VMware Cluster Restoration
Successfully brought the affected ESXi hosts back online, re-established the VMware cluster quorum, and verified the health of the hypervisor environment.

### 🛡️ Data Integrity & Rescue
Ensured zero data loss by safely remounting storage and validating the integrity of highly sensitive customer data, allowing the organization to resume normal operations quickly.

## Impact

Averted a catastrophic data loss scenario and minimized organizational downtime. By quickly resolving the environmental impact and restoring the system within two days, this intervention protected the National Insurance Trust Fund from significant operational, financial, and reputational damage.

## Technologies & Concepts Used

- VMware vSphere / ESXi
- Emergency Hardware Remediation
- Incident Response
- Data Integrity Validation
