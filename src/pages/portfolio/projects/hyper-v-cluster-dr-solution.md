**Client:** Sri Lanka Computer Emergency Readiness Team (SLCERT)  
**Project Duration:** 05/02/2026 – 10/02/2026

## Overview

Designed and deployed a cost-effective Disaster Recovery (DR) solution at a secondary location for SLCERT. Driven by the client's requirement for a budgetary alternative to avoid recent VMware licensing price hikes, the project involved proposing and implementing a highly resilient Microsoft Hyper-V platform. The new DR site was built to mirror the primary site's configuration, ensuring seamless failover and business continuity in the event of a primary site failure.

## Key Features & Contributions

### 🌐 Hyper-V Cluster Deployment
Successfully deployed a 3-node Microsoft Hyper-V cluster running **Windows Server 2025 Standard**, providing a robust and cost-effective virtualization alternative to VMware.

### 🏗️ Hardware Implementation
Installed, configured, and integrated enterprise-grade hardware, specifically utilizing three **Lenovo ThinkSystem SR650 V4** servers paired with a **Lenovo ThinkSystem DE4000H Hybrid Flash Array** for high-performance, reliable storage.

### 🔄 Disaster Recovery Integration
Executed the secure migration and replication of Virtual Machines from the primary datacenter to the new DR environment using **Commvault** backup solutions.

### 💰 Cost Optimization
Delivered a high-value architectural solution that directly addressed the client's financial constraints by strategically pivoting platforms without compromising on high availability or technical capabilities.
